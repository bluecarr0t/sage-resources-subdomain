## How to find Amazing Glamping in Vermont

So, what exactly is glamping, and why do we love it so? Glamping is for people who enjoy spending time outdoors but are less thrilled with sleeping on the ground.

A perfect glamping experience offers comfy accommodations in an outdoor setting, so you get perks like a real bed, a source of heat, and a kitchen, making it the perfect getaway for nature lovers.

You can still enjoy hiking trails, campfires, and relaxing outdoors, and then take advantage of some added comforts. See why we love it?

I use several online resources when searching for the best glamping near me. Here’s a quick overview of the booking platforms that we use frequently:

- **VRBO** – We are still waiting for **[VRBO](https://www.tkqlhce.com/click-100351105-11553823?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.vrbo.com%2F)** to update its online filter to include glamping and camping, but you can search for cabins, cottages, and castles! We have found a lot of hidden gems on VRBO, and many of them are quite affordable.
- **Hipcamp** – [**Hipcamp**](https://www.hipcamp.com/en-US?avad=295505_c452b0695) specializes in all types of private campsites, not just glamping in Vermont. Properties are usually quite rustic and affordable, and there are a lot of dog-friendly glampsites to choose from. If you are new to Hipcamp, use our **[referral link](http://hipcamp.com/i/taras0ccf17)** to get $10 in Hipcash for your first stay.
- **Airbnb** – If you navigate to the **[Airbnb website](https://www.airbnb.com/),** you can quickly click on an icon on the top menu bar to find all kinds of cool glamping sites, including domes, yurts, tiny homes, and A-frames. Airbnb is one of the most popular booking sites for a reason!

### Camp Greenwood in Chester, Vermont

Sleeps: 2

Dogs: yes

Price: $180+

![Sage and Thistle tiny homes at Camp Greenwood in Chester, Vermont.](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/camp-greenwood-vt.jpg)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F01%2Fcamp-greenwood-vt.jpg&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

Sage and Thistle tiny homes at Camp Greenwood in Chester, Vermont

**[Camp Greenwood](https://www.jdoqocy.com/click-100351105-11553823?url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.vrbo.com%2F4328262)** is a beautiful dog-friendly glamping retreat featuring two tiny homes in a central location just outside Chester, Vermont’s historic village.

Sage and Thistle tiny homes can be rented individually, and while they share some amenities, like the beautiful private pond and barrel sauna, each home offers plenty of privacy.

Enjoy a nature-inspired Vermont getaway and explore tiny living while enjoying these amenities:

- A loft bedroom with a queen bed featuring a Casper mattress
- Additional loft for lounging with Smart TV and retro Nintendo mini system
- Full kitchen with stove and oven, fridge + freezer, microwave, farmhouse sink
- French press coffee maker and kettle (for pour-over coffee + tea)
- Plus a few added luxuries you may not always find in a tiny home … tiled shower, traditional flushing toilet, washer and dryer, high-speed WiFi (300 mbs), Heat and AC
- Outdoor area with Breeo fire pit, Weber charcoal grill, and Adirondack chairs

![Camp Greenwood's tiny home interior](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/camp-greenwood-vt-thistle.jpg)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2024%2F01%2Fcamp-greenwood-vt-thistle.jpg&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

Camp Greenwood’s tiny home interior

**Things to do near Chester**: Visit the historic **[Bartonsville Covered Bridge](https://www.bartonsvillecoveredbridge.com/)**, check out the **[Vermont Country Store](https://www.vermontcountrystore.com/visit-us)** in nearby Rockingham, and hike **[the Lost Mine Trail](http://alltrails.pxf.io/jeyXMb)** through the Chester town forest.

### Song Bird Sangha Yurt in New Haven, Vermont

**Sleeps:** 2+

**Dogs:** no

**Price:** $137+

![A cozy yurt in New Haven, Vermont.](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/04/IMG_7112-1.jpg)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2022%2F04%2FIMG_7112-1.jpg&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

This cozy yurt is just outside of Bristol, Vermont

Welcome to **[Song Bird Sangha yurt in New Haven, Vermont](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/5362651?source_impression_id=p3_1705423163_aISFMSdtYevvOOWP)**, just outside of Bristol. This beautiful yurt sleeps two in the well-appointed loft and includes a full kitchen, a pellet stove for chilly nights, a bathroom with a claw-foot tub, and a cozy living room and dining area.

The location makes this woodland yurt feel more like a camping destination — it is nestled in the woods on a 23-acre farm. The outdoor seating area and fire pit allow you to relax after a day of hiking or exploring.

While you have ample opportunities to enjoy the outdoors during your visit, you can cook your meals and sleep in comfort.

During my stay, I could meander around the farm, meet the horses (more on that later), and head into town, which was just a five-minute drive away. Ellen, my host for the weekend, made sure I had everything I needed for a relaxing stay and was a great resource for finding things to do and places to eat around town.

![Yurt interior kitchen.](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/yurt-kitchen-vt.jpg)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2021%2F03%2Fyurt-kitchen-vt.jpg&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

Beautiful kitchen in the yurt

Ellen also hosts a **[horse experience through Airbnb](https://www.airbnb.com/experiences/288936)** — meet the horses and learn how to integrate their wisdom to live more grounded and balanced through mindful techniques and direct interaction with them.

**Things to do near Bristol, Vermont**: Bristol has so many great waterfalls and hiking trails! The **[hike to Bristol Ledges](https://alltrails.pxf.io/4PnXno)** is two miles round-trip and features gorgeous views at the top. Afterward, head over to Bartlett Falls for a dip — it’s one of our favorite **[swimming holes in all of Vermont](https://vermontexplored.com/best-vermont-swimming-holes/)**.

If you don’t want to cook, be sure to make reservations at **[the Bobcat Cafe in downtown Bristol](https://www.thebobcatcafe.com/)**. You will not be disappointed!

### The Dome at Quarry Brook Farm in Putney, Vermont

**Sleeps**: 2+

**Dogs:** yes

**Price:** $145+

![a geodesic dome in Putney Vermont, covered with snow](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/winter-glamping-vt-putney-6.jpg)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2021%2F02%2Fwinter-glamping-vt-putney-6.jpg&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

Enjoying this magical dome on a winter day in Vermont

We discovered this **[Vermont glamping gem](https://www.hipcamp.com/en-US/land/vermont-quarry-brook-gozxhylw?adults=1&children=0&avad=295505_c452b07b1)** on Hipcamp. If you don’t know about Hipcamp, it’s a booking platform that lets you find unique, eclectic, and private camping and glamping spots across the United States.

This little home-away-from-home in Putney is everything you could hope for when glamping in Vermont — it’s private, cozy, and situated on 20 secluded acres with lots of trails for snowshoeing and hiking.

The interior of the dome is the aesthetic of every glamping retreat I’ve ever fallen in love with — lots of open space, plenty of light and warmth, and a super cozy bed next to a roaring woodstove.

![Inside the geodesic dome in Putney. ](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/02/winter-glamping-vt-putney-11.jpg)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2021%2F02%2Fwinter-glamping-vt-putney-11.jpg&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

The beautiful interior of the geodesic dome at Quarry Brook Farm.

It’s very simple and very inviting. More importantly, it is warm. As long as we kept the woodstove going during our stay, we were toasty, though mornings were cool because we let the fire die down overnight.

Dogs are permitted here, which we always appreciate, but they do have two dogs of their own and chickens, too, so keep that in mind.

**Things to do near Putney, Vermont:** There are lots of trails to explore right on the property around the dome, but you should also check out Putney Mountain, which is part of a huge and elaborate trail system managed by the **[Putney Mountain Association](http://putneymountain.org/)** and the [**Windmill Hill Pinnacle Association**.](http://www.windmillhillpinnacle.org/)

**[Read more about this magical glamping dome in Putney, Vermont](https://vermontexplored.com/winter-glamping-putney-vermont/)**

[See it on Hipcamp](https://www.hipcamp.com/en-US/land/vermont-quarry-brook-gozxhylw?adults=1&children=0&avad=295505_c452b07b1)

### Tanglebloom Cabin in Brookline, Vermont

Sleeps: 2

Dogs: no

Price: $263+

![Tanglebloom cabin at night. ](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2021/03/tanglebloom-farm-glamping-vermont.jpg)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2021%2F03%2Ftanglebloom-farm-glamping-vermont.jpg&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

Tanglebloom Cabin after dark

If ever there was an idyllic setting for a romantic Vermont getaway, **[Tanglebloom Flower Farm](https://vermontcabin.co/)** has to be it!

Tanglebloom’s host, Melissa, grows a variety of flowers on a sunny acre surrounded by trees in Southern Vermont, not far from the **[bustling town of Brattleboro](https://vermontexplored.com/things-to-do-in-brattleboro-vt/)**. Just paces from the rows of colorful blooms is a secret path that meanders into the woods.

The rustic, open-air sleeping cabin is just big enough for a bed, a small table, and a chair, yet it is airy and open to the sights, sounds, and smells of the surrounding forest.

Tanglebloom cabin features an outdoor fire pit, table, kitchen area, private outhouse, outdoor shower, and wood-fired hot tub.

Relax near a crackling fire, take in the peekaboo view, or meander through the woods as the sun sinks behind the trees. A night at Tanglebloom is truly good for the soul.

**Things to do near Brookline, Vermont**: There’s an awesome swimming hole under the West Dummerston Covered Bridge, about 6 miles south of the cabin. Head into Brattleboro and visit the galleries and check out the **[Brattleboro Museum & Art Center](https://www.brattleboromuseum.org/)**.

[See it on Airbnb](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/17072599?source_impression_id=p3_1616088370_2R9KVw6ITbLZZ6rR)

Moose Meadow Treehouse in Duxbury, Vermont

**[Moose Meadow Treehouse](https://www.moosemeadowlodge.net/treehouse)** is part of Moose Meadow Lodge, an incredible bed & breakfast that somehow manages to be both rustic and incredibly luxurious.

Located just outside of Waterbury, Vermont, this is the spot you want to go for a little romance, and it’s perfect for a honeymoon suite, an anniversary celebration, or simply a magical night away.

This is one of the most luxurious **[treehouse rentals in Vermont](https://vermontexplored.com/vermont-treehouse-glamping/)**, and it makes an enchanting, next-level getaway for a special occasion!

Nestled in the woods next to a small pond, the unique touches to this treehouse are the ones you’ll never forget. An outdoor shower gives you unparalleled views of the mountains, and a gas stove will keep you toasty on chilly evenings.

Located in Chester, Southern Vermont, **[Zen of the Woods](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/47468969?source_impression_id=p3_1779124148_P3P5np2FXI7U0dgo)** is a cozy, thoughtfully designed tiny cabin retreat minutes from Okemo Mountain.

### The Little Dipper in Underhill

Sleeps: 2

Dog: Yes

Price: $140+

![](https://vermontexplored.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/little-dipper-underhill-airbnb.avif)[Pin](https://pinterest.com/pin/create/bookmarklet/?media=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fwp-content%2Fuploads%2F2025%2F10%2Flittle-dipper-underhill-airbnb.avif&url=https%3A%2F%2Fvermontexplored.com%2Fbest-glamping-in-vermont%2F&is_video=false&description=13%20Enchanting%20Spots%20to%20Go%20Glamping%20in%20Vermont "Pin")

The Little Dipper in Underhill. Photo credit: Airbnb

At just 150 square feet, the Little Dipper is proof that good things come in small packages. This adorable tiny cabin is tucked behind a grove of birch trees on a private nine-acre property in Underhill, and while it may be small, the hosts have thought of just about everything.

You’ll sleep on a queen-size memory foam mattress, cook on a propane stovetop with a fully stocked kitchen (there’s EVOO, balsamic, and an interesting spice collection), and shower outside under the open sky with ONEKA botanical products. Even the outhouse is fully plumbed, a detail the hosts are rightfully proud of (and it makes all the difference when you’re doing your business outdoors).

When you’re not exploring the property or stargazing from the hammock, **[Burlington](https://vermontexplored.com/things-to-do-in-burlington-vt/)** is just 35 minutes away, making this a great base if you want to mix a little city energy into your nature getaway.

A few things to know before you book: there’s no Wi-Fi (hotspot works fine), the sleeping loft is accessed by a ladder, and it’s a short three-minute walk from the parking area to the cabin. The path is well-maintained, and a wheelbarrow is on hand for luggage, but bring a headlamp for after dark.

**Things to do around Underhill:** Be sure to stop at **[Poorhouse Pies](https://www.poorhousepies.com/)** in Underhill (Pie fixes everything) and **[Palmer Hill Maple](https://www.facebook.com/Palmerlanemaple)** in Jericho for a maple creemee. I also recommend checking out the Snowflake Bentley exhibit and the cool gift shop at the **[Old Red Mill in Jericho](https://www.jerichohistoricalsociety.org/old-red-mill.html)**.

[See it on Airbnb](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/1166856572433110642)

**Sleeps:** 6

**Dogs:** yes

This yurt is two miles from Kingdom Trails in Burke. Photo source: Airbnb

Explore the 100+ miles of multi-use trails recognized as the country’s best mountain biking. This off-grid **[20-foot yurt in Burke](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/16980735?source_impression_id=p3_1705584329_xbUa9PQRo6cDB4Sn)** can comfortably accommodate six people, with additional room for tents outside. The yurt is just two miles from Kingdom Trails and a short drive from Burke Mountain. Located on 38 private acres and dog-friendly, this off-grid paradise is the perfect spot for anyone who wants to explore more of the Northeast Kingdom in Vermont.

**Things to do near Burke:** Ride or hike **[the Kingdom Trails](https://www.kingdomtrails.org/)**, ski at **[Burke Mountain](https://www.skiburke.com/)**, or take a dip in beautiful Lake Willoughby.
