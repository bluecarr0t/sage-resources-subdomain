# OUR VISION

Entrada Camps and Conservation is dedicated to setting new standards for sustainability, service and luxury for the hospitality industry in the American West. Inspired by the iconic luxury safari camps of Africa, our tented resorts will be located in some of the world's most dramatically beautiful natural settings and will provide guests with deeply immersive and transformational experiences.

Our model of low-impact, high value tourism is designed to provide real economic benefits to local communities and people while minimizing the negative effects of tourism growth on already over-touristed places.

## Sustainability





Our tented camps are designed and built to have the least possible impact on the land and surrounding environments. With a maximum of just 16 guest tents per camp, we strive to minimize the use of hard infrastructure so that all facilities may eventually be removed without a trace. We employ cutting edge technologies to generate sustainable energy, minimize waste and our energy and water needs.

- ![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/611ce51deb451c516e18cc5a/1629369173471-BC5II0QD04KOFS54JMOC/leaf.png)







## Conservation & Stewardship





Wildlife and habitat conservation are at the heart of everything we do. A crucial tenet of our mission is to acquire and protect large tracts of land in sensitive natural areas and to preserve critical wildlife habitat. And through our non-profit Entrada Foundation, we will donate 1% of our gross revenue to support community conservation initiatives and will actively educate our guests about local conservation issues and provide them with a mechanism to donate themselves.

- ![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/611ce51deb451c516e18cc5a/1629369200934-6MTGD9MWUQPP1AMIPUE6/community.png)







## Community & People





We are deeply committed to being good. engaged citizens of the communities where we operate. As such, we strive to contribute meaningfully to local economies, support local people and enhance their quality of life. Our model of low-impact, high value tourism (a small number of rooms sold at a very high rate) ensures that we will have minimal impact on the overall visitor capacity of our destinations, while allowing us to contribute significantly to the local tax base and to pay above market salaries and benefits to our staff.

## FEATURED PROJECT: ENTRADA MOAB

Entrada Moab will be North American's first stand-alone luxury tented camp inspired by the luxury safari camps of Africa.

Nestled within a private and secluded 550-acre private reserve the very heart of Utah's legendary redrock canyon country, Entrada Moab will offer guests an intimate and luxurious base from which to explore two world-class national parks and millions of acres of exquisitely beautiful public lands and to experience world-class hiking, mountain biking river rafting and more.

## WHO WE ARE

Entrada Camps and Conservation was founded by experts in luxury adventure travel and pioneers in sustainable tourism. We are united by the common commitment to the idea that travel, at its best, can be a profoundly positive force for good in the world; one that romotes the conservation of our shared natural heritage, enhances and nourishes communities, and connects people

- ## Matt Kareus





Matt is a 25-year veteran of the luxury adventure travel and African safari industries and has served in senior leadership positions for some of the top natural history and wildlife tour operators in the world.



As the vice president of marketing at Natural Habitat Adventures (NHA) from 2001 to 2009, he spearheaded several innovative and award-winning sustainability and conservation initiatives. Under his leadership, NHA became the world's first carbon neutral tour operator in 2008 and was named the world's greenest tour operator by Conde Nast Traveler magazine. He also forged a unique partnership with WWF-US which has since yielded over $500m in conservation funding.



Since 2010, he has served in key leadership positions in non-profit organizations dedicated to promoting sustainable tourism in some of the world's most ecologically sensitive and popular tourist destinations, including the International Galapagos Tour Operators Association, Sustainable Travel International and Friends of Serengeti.
