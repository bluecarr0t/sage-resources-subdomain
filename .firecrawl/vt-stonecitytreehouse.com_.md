# ﻿& Glamping

**A secluded Micro-retreat in the woods of Hardwick, Vermont**

Escape to a serene, nature-infused retreat in the heart of the northern Vermont woods, where artisan, rustic charm meets the great outdoors. Our glamping sanctuary offers a relaxing, magical experience with a thoughtfully-crafted treehouse, a canvas bell tent, a unique and eclectic home, and a forest sauna with gathering circles and a year-round stream.

Green Up Day in Vermont is often shared as a feel-good event, and in many ways it is.

But also every year I get so angry, and suspicion arises from me with every car that drives by - are they the one that is throwing their Arnold Palmer, Natural Light, or Twisted Tea, or even a full bag of household trash, into the woods for me, a 53 year old woman, to pick up?

Green Up Day is the paradox of humans - us at our best, helping the Earth and each other, and humans showing complete disregard for the Earth and their neighbors.

This year things were looking better than most years overall, but I wonder if someone went before me and cleaned things up.

People with no consideration of the Earth and the neighbors seem to like to throw things down hills into the woods. Do they think it just disappears? I hope they don’t it intentionally, because it is much more difficult to clean up.

I found a few containers of dirty plates and pots and silverware. Did they just not feel like cleaning it up and thought throwing it down a hill into the woods was a better way to deal with it?

There was another spot on my neighbor’s property where an enormous amount of household trash was thrown really far down a steep hill. It will take a group to clean that up.

There was a plastic bag with suet wrappers thrown into the woods 😶 Really?

Still, there is the contrast of the beautiful springtime woodland flowers, still persisting among the human-made mess.

I’m tired of human-made messes. What is wrong with us? I have been feeling so much despair and anger lately because of state of world.

When will we learn? I want a different world, with the same Earth. Can’t we please do better? It breaks my heart 💔

Here I am (in this picture!) at one of my happy places. I’m at an amazing treehouse @volcanotreehousebnb near Kilauea volcano on the Big Island of Hawaii.

Most of my selfies I have what I call “resting sad face” 😂 but here I don’t because I’m in one of the most magical places on earth (besides @stonecityvermont ) with a man that I adore.

I have so much gratitude for many of the opportunities that I’ve had and the people in my life. But there are things that I struggle with, like we all do

I was reading today in a book called Regenerative Business by @thedirtyalchemy that those things that really trigger us are those things that we are trying to heal in our lives. This is our unconscious bringing attention to it.

A few of the major things that I see in me recently are being very sensitive to what I perceive as me not doing the best possible job or holding up what I say that I will do. Not living up to the expectations of the people I care about and love and disappointing them. It is very important to me to please the people I love and be there for them in every way, so it hurts when I hear that I haven’t done this. And I also know that I often fail to meet this mark and I get defensive about it sometimes.

The other thing is that I take things very personally. While I’ve gotten better at this, it still is a struggle for me. I think it ties back to my previous statement.

So this book has suggested that I embrace those reactions that I have and love them as a way to healing. This makes sense to me on one level, but how do I embrace the things that I like the least?

What do you struggle with, if you want to share? What do you think about this idea?
