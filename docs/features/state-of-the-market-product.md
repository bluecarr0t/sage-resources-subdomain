# State of the Market: Repeatable Research Product

Strategic outline for a Sage Outdoor Advisory **repeatable “state of the market”** offering: regional and national briefs, differentiated cuts for feasibility and asset-level decisions, and a phased rollout that preserves credibility as sample size and data partnerships grow.

**Related context:** Industry conferences and platforms increasingly position **occupancy, trends, and statistical analysis** as core education (e.g., association conventions, long-running camping research, large booking platforms). Sage can compete not on raw transaction scale alone but on **independence, methodology, feasibility-relevant segmentation, and capital-markets-grade clarity**.

---

## 1. Core concept

Publish **regional and national briefs** (e.g., Florida/Alabama, Southeast, Sun Belt, national rollup) with:

- **Clear methodology** (definitions, sources, aggregation rules).
- **Explicit sample limits** (minimum-N thresholds, what is excluded).
- **“What changed vs. last quarter”** (or last period) as a fixed section—even when moves are small—to build habit and trust.

**Mirror the industry hook** where it helps distribution:

- Occupancy and occupancy analogs.
- ADR / RevPAR-style analogs for outdoor hospitality (defined consistently).
- Booking lead times, length of stay, seasonality.

**Differentiate** with cuts that match **feasibility and underwriting**, not only marketing dashboards:

- **Glamping vs. RV** (and hybrid parks where labeled).
- **Public vs. private** (or agency vs. independent) where data allows.
- **Drive-to vs. fly-to** (feeder-market and trip-length logic, not only state lines).
- Optional: **absorption proxies**, **rate architecture** (nightly vs. weekly, minimum stays), **capex / amenity pressure** (survey + benchmarks).

---

## 2. Different product shapes (same spine, different wrappers)

These can share one data pipeline and one metric dictionary; packaging changes by audience and channel.

| Product | Cadence / form | Purpose |
|--------|----------------|--------|
| **Sage Market Pulse** | Monthly or quarterly PDF + landing page | Short (e.g., 8–12 pages), charts-first, one headline truth per region, three operator takeaways, one investor/lender takeaway. **Twist:** confidence badges (high / medium / emerging) per chart from sample and stability. |
| **Sage Regional Deck** | Slide kit | Conference, broker, and lender use; appendix with methodology and FAQs (“why isn’t this my PMS?”). **Twist:** two scenario panels—base vs. stress (weather, fuel band, soft macro). |
| **Sage Segment Monitors** | Rotating deep dives | Glamping, RV resorts, municipal/agency campgrounds, hybrid parks, event-driven markets. **Twist:** each ends with **feasibility implications** (common pro forma mistakes in that segment). |
| **Sage Feasibility Lens** | Section inside Pulse or standalone | Decision metrics: absorption proxies, pipeline/permit signals where available, capex pressure index (survey + cost benchmarks), feeder-market concentration vs. fly-to flywheel. |
| **Sage Lender Pack** | Quarterly, shorter | Fewer indicators; definitions aligned to underwriting; explicit “do not use for…” guardrails. |
| **Sage Association Brief** | White-label 2-pager + backup slides | State/regional associations as distribution. **Twist:** local callouts (events, seasonality, weather windows) without overfitting noise. |
| **Sage Data Room Light** | Subscriber bundle | Aggregated indices CSV, chart assets, glossary, **versioned** changelog (`2026Q1-v1.1`) for IC memos and citations. |
| **“What changed” memo** | One-pager / email | Five bullets, three charts, “what we’re watching next month.” Trains the market on **your** release calendar. |

## 3. Core modules (standard table of contents)

Standardize early; vary depth by phase.

| Module | Purpose |
|--------|--------|
| **Universe & sample** | Inclusion/exclusion, refresh cadence, lag rules. |
| **Definitions** | Occupancy analog, ADR/RevPAR analog, lead time, LOS—single source of truth. |
| **Regional cuts** | State, metro, drive-time bands, tourism districts where possible. |
| **Segment cuts** | Glamping vs. RV, private vs. public, premium vs. economy proxies. |
| **Seasonality** | Index vs. raw; holiday effects; optional event overlays. |
| **Forward-looking proxies** | On-the-books pace if available; else search/lead indicators + survey—labeled clearly. |
| **Supply & competition** | New supply, expansions, entrant rate (quantitative or qualitative in early phases). |
| **Feasibility appendix** | How operators and modelers should **not** misread the charts. |

## 4. Differentiation angles

1. **Asset-class translation:** Express outdoor hospitality metrics in **capital-markets language** (trailing vs. forward, quality of earnings, seasonality risk).
2. **Segment honesty:** Publish **minimum-N thresholds**; prefer blank or “emerging” over spurious precision.
3. **Dual interpretation:** Same dataset—**operator layer** and **investor/lender layer**.
4. **Belief checks:** One recurring page testing conventional wisdom (“what owners believe vs. what the data supports”)—strong for conference narratives and press.
5. **Geo logic for feasibility:** Drive-to feeder markets and trip-length buckets, not only political boundaries.

**Positioning line (internal):** *Platforms know transactions; Sage knows markets*—double down on independence and methodology while pursuing distribution (associations, press, recurring indices).

---

## 5. Phased approach

### Phase 0 — Foundations (about 2–4 weeks)

- **Metric dictionary** (terms and formulas locked).
- **Release calendar** (e.g., monthly delta memo vs. quarterly deep brief).
- **Data governance:** aggregation rules, privacy posture, contributor rules if any.
- **Design system:** template, chart style, footnote standard.

**Exit criteria:** One-sentence product description; every metric defensible on one page.

### Phase 1 — Credible v1 (about 4–8 weeks)

- Launch **one flagship region** (e.g., Southeast or FL/AL if aligned to marketing moments).
- Ship **3–5 indicators** that can be supported well (examples: seasonality index, LOS band, lead-time band, rate mix proxy, segment split).
- Ship **methodology PDF** + sample description + confidence badges.
- Include **what changed vs. last period** every release.

**Exit criteria:** Reception is “conservative but real,” not “flashy but suspicious.”

### Phase 2 — Productize the bundle (about 2–3 quarters)

- Expand to **Sun Belt + national rollup** with explicit hierarchy (national summary ≠ local truth).
- **Segment Monitors** on a rotating schedule.
- **Lender Pack** and **Association Brief** formats for distribution.
- **Changelog** and **revision policy** for subscribers and citations.

**Exit criteria:** Repeatable production pipeline; predictable ship dates; inbound from brokers/associations.

### Phase 3 — Moat-building (12–24 months; parallel tracks)

- **Contributor / co-op** model for richer cuts (aggregated only).
- **Partnerships** with platforms or PMS ecosystems without becoming vendor-tied analytics.
- **Named benchmark indices** with stable methodology (illustrative names: demand index, rate pressure index).
- **API / data room** tier for paid subscribers.

**Exit criteria:** Indices referenced in third-party decks and articles without Sage in the room.

---

## 6. Operating model

- **Editorial lead:** narrative, claims risk, plain-English interpretation.
- **Methods lead:** definitions, QA, sample integrity, robust stats (medians, trimmed/winsorized means as appropriate—disclosed).
- **Production cadence:** fixed week per month or quarter.
- **External review (optional):** occasional academic or third-party methodology review to increase citation trust.

---

## 7. Early pitfalls to avoid

- **Hyper-local slices** before N supports them.
- **Mixing transactional and survey metrics** without clear labeling.
- **Outlier-driven regional lines**—use robust aggregation and document it.
- **Overclaiming** when sample is thin—prefer “emerging” and wider bands.

---

## 8. Optional 90-day wedge

1. One **public** flagship regional brief with rigorous caveats.  
2. One **myth-busting** or **belief-check** piece with primary survey data if available.  
3. One **association or media** partnership for distribution.  
4. A **methodology PDF** that others can link and cite.
